Step 1 - Request for Service

This “Request for Service” step is the first step of the {ot-practice-process-link}. It is important as it sets the scene for how you will start to work in this particular practice area and with this particular person(s). You already know your practice context - where you are working and the people you will be working with (the other disciplines and the clients). Decisions you make now will set you on one particular course of action. This can always be added to or altered along the way. This is your actual starting point.

Audio: Practice Reasoning

These audio clips feature three pairs of occupational therapy students demonstrating using their practice reasoning at the Request for Service Step.

In this exercise, each pair of students was given the set of de-identified request for service (referral) forms seen here:

  • Standard Intake Form

  • Occupational Therapy Outpatient Referral Form – Adult or Child

  • Occupational Therapy Community Referral Form

and asked talk through their thinking about the client together, as they looked at the forms.

Toward the end, they were also given a photo of the client to help their thinking.

Each pair of students is at a different competency development level:

  • Emerging

  • Consolidating

  • Competent to Graduate

The progression of practice reasoning and understanding is evident at each development level.

These examples will be useful to you in thinking about your own practice reasoning and in looking at a request for service (referral).
