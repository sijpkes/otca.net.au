Personal & Professional Growth

To be a really effective therapist you have to use yourself as a tool in therapy. In the literature this is referred to as: “the professional use of self”. To do this, you need to be very aware of yourself. This ability will give you greater choices about how you respond to the people you work with. Below you will find some self-awareness resources to help you get to know yourself better. Getting and using feedback from others will add to this. Knowing, managing and caring for yourself is a great foundation for working effectively with your clients and colleagues during your placements, throughout your career and through your life-long learning journey.

Self-Relfection

You cannot reflect on yourself unless you are aware of yourself so try the self-awareness exercises in the Personal & Professional Growth tab first. Being reflective about yourself and your work is necessary for all your practice placement supervision and Continuing Professional Education (CPE) requirements as it gives you greater insight into your strengths and weaknesses, your personal qualities and patterns of behaviour that make up your professional identity. To do this you can use your personal and professional experiences to understand them more deeply and to build on them. Part of these professional experiences, includes the application of your knowledge and the currency of that evidence. Reflecting on all these factors will help you develop your professional self.
